Creating Professional Email with Your Domain

üìñ 8 min read üëÅÔ∏è 18.3k views üìÖ Updated Dec 2025

Having a professional email address with your own domain name (like info@yourdomain.com instead of yourname@gmail.com) adds credibility to your business and helps build trust with customers. This comprehensive guide will show you how to create professional email accounts using cPanel.

Why Use Professional Email?

  • Brand Credibility: Shows you're a legitimate business
  • Professional Image: Makes better impression than free email services
  • Custom Addresses: Create unlimited addresses like sales@, support@, info@
  • Better Control: Full ownership and control of your email data
  • Enhanced Security: Advanced spam filtering and virus protection included

Prerequisites

  • Active AxiomHost hosting account
  • Domain name added to your hosting account
  • cPanel login credentials

Step-by-Step: Creating Email Accounts

Step 1: Access cPanel Email Accounts

  1. Log into your cPanel at yourdomain.com/cpanel
  2. Scroll down to the Email section
  3. Click on Email Accounts

Step 2: Create a New Email Account

  1. Click the + Create button at the top right
  2. The email account creation form will appear

Step 3: Configure Email Account Details

Username

Enter the part before the @ symbol. Common examples:

  • info - for general inquiries (info@yourdomain.com)
  • support - for customer support (support@yourdomain.com)
  • sales - for sales team (sales@yourdomain.com)
  • your.name - personal email (john.doe@yourdomain.com)
üí° Pro Tip: Use descriptive email addresses that clearly indicate their purpose. This makes it easier for customers to know who they're contacting.

Domain

Select your domain from the dropdown menu. If you have multiple domains, make sure to select the correct one.

Password

Create a strong password for this email account:

  • At least 12 characters long
  • Mix of uppercase and lowercase letters
  • Include numbers and special characters
  • Avoid dictionary words or personal information
‚ö†Ô∏è Security Warning: Never use the same password for multiple email accounts. Each account should have a unique, strong password to prevent unauthorized access.

Storage Space

Set the mailbox quota (storage limit). Options:

  • Unlimited: No storage restrictions (recommended for most users)
  • Custom Quota: Set specific MB limit (useful for managing disk space)
üìä Recommended Settings: For most business users, set to "Unlimited" or at least 2000 MB (2 GB) to prevent mailbox full errors.

Step 4: Create the Email Account

  1. Review all information for accuracy
  2. Click the + Create button
  3. You'll see a success message confirming the account was created

Accessing Your Email

Option 1: Webmail (Browser-Based)

Access your email from any browser without installing software:

  1. Navigate to yourdomain.com/webmail
  2. Enter your full email address (e.g., info@yourdomain.com)
  3. Enter the password you created
  4. Click Log in
  5. Choose between three webmail clients:
    • Roundcube: Modern interface, recommended for most users
    • Horde: Feature-rich with calendar and tasks
    • SquirrelMail: Lightweight and simple
üí° Recommended: Use Roundcube for the best user experience. It has a modern interface similar to Gmail and includes all essential features.

Option 2: Email Client (Desktop/Mobile)

Configure your email on Outlook, Thunderbird, Apple Mail, or mobile devices.

Email Configuration Settings

You'll need these settings to configure your email client:

Incoming Mail Server (IMAP - Recommended):
Server: mail.yourdomain.com
Port: 993
Security: SSL/TLS
Username: info@yourdomain.com
Password: [your email password]

Incoming Mail Server (POP3 - Alternative):
Server: mail.yourdomain.com
Port: 995
Security: SSL/TLS
Username: info@yourdomain.com
Password: [your email password]

Outgoing Mail Server (SMTP):
Server: mail.yourdomain.com
Port: 465 (SSL) or 587 (TLS)
Security: SSL/TLS
Authentication: Required
Username: info@yourdomain.com
Password: [your email password]
üì± IMAP vs POP3: IMAP (recommended) keeps emails on the server and syncs across all devices. POP3 downloads emails to one device and removes them from the server. Choose IMAP for multi-device access.

Managing Email Accounts

Changing Email Password

  1. Go to Email Accounts in cPanel
  2. Find the email account you want to modify
  3. Click Manage next to the account
  4. Click Change Password
  5. Enter new password and click Change Password

Checking Disk Usage

  1. In Email Accounts, view the usage bar next to each account
  2. Green = healthy usage
  3. Yellow/Red = approaching/exceeded quota
  4. Click Manage to see detailed usage statistics

Deleting Email Accounts

  1. In Email Accounts, find the account to delete
  2. Click Delete
  3. Confirm deletion (this action cannot be undone)
‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Deleting an email account permanently removes all emails, folders, and settings. Make sure to backup important emails before deletion.

Setting Up Email Forwarders

Email forwarders automatically redirect emails from one address to another.

Creating a Forwarder

  1. In cPanel, go to Email section
  2. Click Forwarders
  3. Click Add Forwarder
  4. Enter the address to forward FROM (e.g., sales@yourdomain.com)
  5. Enter the address to forward TO (e.g., john@yourdomain.com or john@gmail.com)
  6. Click Add Forwarder
üí° Use Case Example: Forward contact@yourdomain.com to your personal Gmail to check messages on the go, while maintaining a professional email address for customers.

Configuring Auto-Responders

Auto-responders send automatic replies when you receive emails. Perfect for vacation messages or acknowledgment emails.

Setting Up Auto-Responder

  1. In cPanel, go to Email section
  2. Click Auto Responders
  3. Click Add Auto Responder
  4. Select the email address
  5. Enter subject line (e.g., "Out of Office")
  6. Enter your message body
  7. Set start and stop dates (optional)
  8. Click Create/Modify

Best Practices for Professional Email

Security

  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for cPanel
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each email account
  • Regularly update passwords (every 90 days recommended)
  • Never share email passwords
  • Be cautious of phishing emails

Organization

  • Create department-specific addresses (sales@, support@, billing@)
  • Use email filters to automatically organize incoming mail
  • Set up folders for different projects or clients
  • Archive old emails regularly to save space

Deliverability

  • Configure SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records (see Email Authentication Guide)
  • Avoid sending mass emails without proper permission
  • Keep your domain's reputation clean
  • Monitor spam folder regularly

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Can't Send or Receive Emails

Solutions:

  • Verify email account credentials are correct
  • Check mailbox isn't full (exceeding quota)
  • Ensure domain DNS is properly configured
  • Verify mail server settings in your email client
  • Check if your IP is blacklisted

Emails Going to Spam

Solutions:

  • Configure SPF, DKIM, and DMARC authentication
  • Avoid spam trigger words in subject lines
  • Don't send too many emails at once
  • Ask recipients to add you to their contacts

Password Not Working

Solutions:

  • Reset password from cPanel > Email Accounts
  • Ensure you're using the full email address as username
  • Check for typos (passwords are case-sensitive)
  • Clear browser cache and cookies
